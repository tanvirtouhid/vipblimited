Common Questions

Frequently
Asked

Funds are mobilized through equity, long term loans and third party funds under VIPB's management.

VIPB invests in common equity in private enterprises. Typically it aims for significant minority to majority positions with a defined investment horizon, expected financial returns and impact objectives.

Convertible instruments are those which can be converted optionally or mandatorily once the conversion mechanism triggers.

Quasi equity is an investment product that features elements of a fixed income investment scheme as well as variable income sharing mechanism linked to operating performance parameters of business.

For equity investments, VIPB shares pro rata share of profit or loss. For quasi-equity investments VIPB shares business risks within the framework of revenue/income sharing model of the product.

VIPB has a defined investment horizon of three to five years typically. It exits at the end of the horizon realizing a target return — VIPB does not hold investments indefinitely.
